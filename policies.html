Academic integrity

Students at Vanderbilt are expected to adhere to the Vanderbilt Honor Code. Academic dishonesty, including all forms of cheating, inappropriate use of generative AI, and plagiarism will not be tolerated. If you are found guilty of academic dishonesty, potential consequences range from failing an assignment to being suspended from the University. All forms of academic dishonesty will be reported to the Undergraduate Honor Code Incident Reporting Form.

Discussion with classmates is encouraged. You are not, however, permitted to share notebook files or copy code directly from another student's submission. All code you submit must be written by you, and you must be able to explain every line.

Generative AI

Generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.) is prohibited in this course unless explicit permission is given on the specific assignment.

When generative AI is permitted, you must cite the resource as you would any other source. Failing to acknowledge its use, or using it when not permitted, is academic dishonesty and will be referred to the Honor Council.

If you see a creative opportunity to use generative AI in your work, propose it to Dr. Morey — it will be given fair consideration.

Attendance and participation

Missing class means missing material that has been intentionally curated for your learning. Full attendance and participation is expected. Research shows that students who respond thoughtfully to in-class questions earn higher grades — even when they get the initial answer wrong.

What happens if you miss class? Talk with classmates to get caught up and go over important concepts. If you still have questions, come to office hours. While full attendance is expected, life happens — if you miss an activity or assignment, contact Dr. Morey as soon as possible (ideally before you miss it) so we can try to make a plan.

If you're sick, please do not come to class. Rest for the day. It is far more important that you are safe and healthy than that you attend class.

Athletes

It is your responsibility to contact Dr. Morey when you will miss class for an athletic event — do not rely on your athletic director to communicate it. Include (1) your name, (2) what you'll be missing and when, and (3) a request to make it up if applicable. Do this ahead of time. Note that absences for athletic events are still subject to the regular attendance policy. If you will regularly miss a majority of class sessions due to athletic commitments, you are unfortunately not in the right class.

Graduating seniors

Graduating seniors who are not on track to pass but who would otherwise be eligible to graduate may request a senior reexamination from the Dean's office. The format (oral or mixed-question) is at Dr. Morey's discretion. If you're a senior worried about your ability to succeed — or any student! — meet with Dr. Morey early and often.

Classroom conduct

Everyone in this classroom will be held to the same standard: speak in a way that is not rude, disruptive, intimidating, or demeaning. Disruptive behavior will not be tolerated and may result in being asked to leave or a referral to the Student Honor Council.

In Dr. Morey's eyes, there is as much to learn from you this semester as you have to learn from her. The classroom cannot be conducive to learning if people speak to one another with condescension.

Classroom etiquette

  1. Ask questions. The best part of class is working together to understand the material. If you have a question, you are almost certainly not the only one.
  2. Please do not arrive late or leave early. If unavoidable, minimize disruption to your classmates.
  3. Please use common courtesy when others are speaking. Actively listen with the goal of learning — not just waiting for your turn to talk.
  4. Put your phone on silent and use your computer appropriately. Your attention is one of your greatest assets.

How you start the semester determines how you finish the semester. Show up prepared and ready to actively participate.

Grading irregularities

If you believe there is a mistake in the grading of an assignment, provide a brief written justification (email is fine) along with the original assignment within one week of the return of the graded work. Include image attachments of the assignment so both parties have a record.

To be eligible for an "Incomplete" (I) grade, you must be earning at least a C in the course and must provide written documentation detailing the reason for the grade.

Weather and cancellations

If a class session is canceled due to bad weather, instructor absence, or other unforeseen circumstances, students are still responsible for all readings and assignments listed on the schedule.
